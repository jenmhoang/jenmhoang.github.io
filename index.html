Hello! I'm a sophomore UC Berkeley student who is passionate about software accessibility and the pursual of human rights; I hope to use what I've learned as a Computer Science major to develop technology that can advance public welfare and contribute to social good. I've also been exploring the world of Computer Graphics and 3D Design, where I aim to use my technical skills to automate and enhance graphical tools.

As a Software Developer for Blueprint, a tech consulting group that develops software pro-bono for nonprofit organizations, I am developing a user-friendly application that streamlines the on-boarding process of 1951 Coffee Company's refugee resettlement and education program.

Additionally, I act as a class facilitator for the UC Berkeley Undergraduate Graphics Group, where I teach students how to navigate Autodesk Maya and perform each step of the 3D Animation pipeline.
