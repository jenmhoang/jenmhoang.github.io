1951 Coffee Company

React Native, JavaScript, HTML, CSS

The Problem
Our Solution
Reflection

A mobile application built for efficient
communication and resource provision.

Duration: 3 months (MVP), ongoing project

The Client

1951 Coffee Company, is a nonprofit organization that trains and employs recently resettled refugees/asylees in the Bay Area. Oftentimes, upon initial immigration, refugees are not given the proper support to attain living-wage jobs, as they face the challenges of language barriers and lack of resources. Through their Barista Training Program, 1951 Coffee Company prepares over 100 refugees per year for entrance into the coffee industry, where they are able to maintain successful jobs.

The Team

Blueprint is a tech consulting organization that develops software pro-bono for nonprofit organizations. We aim to create in pursuit of a better world, through provision of accessible technology to in-need communities. Our team consists of four developers (including myself), one designer, and one project leader.

1951 Coffee Company hosts two-week barista training programs throughout the year. Upon completion of the program, a Recruitment Specialist works with graduates to guide them through job opportunities and interview prep so that they can smoothly enter the coffee industry.

The Problem

The group does not have an proper organization or communication platform to efficiently run their program. Any time there is an upcoming event or important message that needs to be relayed to the students, the program admins must manually type in the phone number of each individual trainee, sending the same text message to each participant in their program. The same tedious process occurs when the Recruitment Specialist reaches out to graduates regarding job opportunities, with the text message history acting as the only record of participants' job interests.

Our team created a platform that provides access to community messaging, job opportunities, upcoming class events, and barissta guides. This mobile application is compatible for Android and iOS. As of now, the admin dashboard is built entirely on Airtable, where admins can manage users, send messages, and update information through various forms and tables. As the project progresses, we aim to build a separate webpage for admin usage.

Users

Admins can add new users through through simple input of information onto Airtable. Usernames are automatically created with first name and last name inputs. Users can then log into their accounts through the admin-initialized usernames and passwords upon first opening the application.
