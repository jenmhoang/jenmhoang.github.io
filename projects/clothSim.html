In this project, I implemented a real-time simulation of cloth using a mass and spring system that accurately represents the way cloth moves when pinned, falling, or colliding with other objects/itself.

Masses and Springs

Simulation via Numerical Integration

Changing the ks constant


The spring constant ks measures the stiffness of the springs. At a low ks value, the cloth was more flexible with many wrinkles. At a high ks value, the cloth was stiffer, with smoother folds.

Changing the density


The density value dictates the weight of the cloth. At a high density value, the cloth has a greater downward pull compared to a cloth with a low density value.

Changing the damping value


The damping factor represents all contributions (such as friction, heat loss, etc) to net energy loss in the cloth system. At higher damping values, the spring oscillations decay much faster, bringing the cloth to a resting state faster than at low damping values.

Self-Collisions


At a low density, less weight acts on the cloth, and has large folds, compared to the heavy, small folds that appear at higher density.

At a high ks value, the cloth is stiffer and falls in large, smooth folds, compared to the many small ripples that appear at a lower ks value.
