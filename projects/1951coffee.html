Our Solution

Duration: 6 months

The Client

1951 Coffee Company, is a nonprofit organization that trains and employs recently resettled refugees/asylees in the Bay Area. Oftentimes, upon initial immigration, refugees are not given the proper support to attain living-wage jobs, as they face the challenges of language barriers and lack of resources. Through their Barista Training Program, 1951 Coffee Company prepares over 100 refugees per year for entrance into the coffee industry, where they are able to maintain successful jobs.

The Team

Blueprint is a tech consulting organization that develops software pro-bono for nonprofit organizations. We aim to create in pursuit of a better world, through provision of accessible technology to in-need communities. Our project team consists of four developers (including myself), one designer, and one project leader.

The organization hosts Barista Training Programs throughout the year. Upon completion of the program, a Recruitment Specialist works with graduates to guide them through job opportunities and interview prep so that they can smoothly enter the coffee industry.

The Problem

1951 Coffee Company does not have an proper organization or communication platform to efficiently run their program. Any time there is an upcoming event or important message that needs to be relayed to the students, the program admins must manually type in the phone number of each individual trainee, sending the same text message to each participant in their program. The same tedious process occurs when the Recruitment Specialist reaches out to graduates regarding job opportunities, with the text message history acting as the only record of participants' job interests.

Our team created a platform that provides access to community messaging, job opportunities, upcoming class events, and educational resources. This mobile application is offered on Android and iOS through the App Store. The admin dashboard is built entirely on Airtable, where admins can manage users, send messages, and update information through various forms and tables.

Users

Admins can add new users through simple input of information onto an Airtable database. Usernames are automatically created with first name and last name inputs. Users can then log into their accounts through the admin-initialized usernames and passwords upon first opening the application.

Messaging

Admins can send mass announcements, event details, and messages to multiple cohorts at a time. Users can respond to admins through embedded SMS messaging.

Jobs

After graduating from a Barista Training Program, users will have access to a Jobs screen, where they can view job opportunities and indicate interest on the app. Their interest will be recorded on the admin dashboard, where the Recruitment Specialist will be notified and can help the graduate move forward with the job opportunity.

Classes

A large portion of participants within the program come from community referrals. The Classes screens allows users to view current and upcoming classes and their corresponding events, from where they can view their own schedule as well as recommend future program events to friends.

Resource Guide

The application also includes a Resources screen that includes class material that students could reference during or after their program. Resources include a barista brew guide, interview prep, coffee-making practice worksheets, and links to video tutorials. All resources are drawn from the Airtable database and can be easily updated and changed by admins.
